# Quality Assurance

The Shepherd Research Lab serves as a DXA (dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry) quality-assurance site for NIH-funded studies, CDC research programs, and other large multi-site studies. Dr. Shepherd holds the patent that Hologic uses for body-composition measurement and has served as past president of the ISCD, participating in every ISCD Position Development Conference.

## Technical infrastructure

The lab maintains workstations for both Hologic and GE systems with rights to use their software on external scans. Our QA work involves:

- Reviewing and revising DXA image data to ensure consistency by defining regions of interest within and among participants
- Harmonizing data from Lunar DXA systems with subsequent Hologic scanner acquisitions through cross-calibration equations and longitudinal corrections
- Performing quality-control checks on participant alignment, region positioning, and DXA-scan accuracy
- Defining protocols for consistency within and among participants' scans
- Establishing procedures for continued QC, reanalysis, and data generation — with site manuals and training materials

## Current QA engagements

### NHANES 2018–2022

Analysis and quality-control review of DXA scans (hip, spine, VFA spine, whole body) at mobile examination centers, including longitudinal calibrations and cross-calibrations.

### IMPAACT 2009 & 2010

Monitoring bone-mineral-density changes through central analysis of spine, femur, and whole-body DXA scans in HIV prevention and treatment studies.

### HANDLS

DXA analysis and quality control for body-composition and bone-mineral-density data examining health disparities across socioeconomic groups in Baltimore.

### Extended use of NHANES Visceral Adiposity Cancer Risk Factors to GE Systems

Determining relationships between Hologic and GE systems for visceral-adiposity measurements; providing cutoff points for cancer and metabolic risk.
