# Join Us

The Shepherd Research Lab, led by Dr. John A. Shepherd at the University of Hawaiʻi Cancer Center, develops innovative methodology for multimodality image analysis with clinical applications. We especially seek individuals with computer-science backgrounds and expertise in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and deep learning.

## Working with SRL

The lab welcomes people of all backgrounds and operates as a dynamic, cohesive unit. Dr. Shepherd provides resources and professional connections to help researchers meet career goals, with supervision styles tailored to individual needs.

## Graduate-student philosophy

Dr. Shepherd expects graduate students to be hardworking and passionate — with opportunities to write grants, publish in peer-reviewed journals, present at conferences, and collaborate across campus.

> "Do I expect your graduate research to consume every aspect of your life? Definitely no. Do I expect you to be committed to and excited about your work? Absolutely yes!"

## Open positions

### Postdoctoral Fellows — Position ID 223315 (via RCUH Jobs Board)

We're recruiting bright, motivated postdoctoral fellows pursuing academic careers in radiomics and cancer research. Responsibilities: leading research workflows, developing imaging biomarkers, data analysis, manuscript preparation, conference presentations, and mentoring graduate students.

**Key qualifications**

- PhD in Bioengineering, Computer Science, Physics, or a related field
- 0–1 year experience in imaging data analysis, computer vision, or data science
- Proficiency in at least one programming language (Python, MATLAB, R, C/C++)
- Strong quantitative analytical and communication skills
- Ability to pass a background check and complete required training (CITI, HIPAA/PHI, bloodborne-pathogen standards)

**How to apply**

1. Visit the [RCUH Jobs Board ↗](https://hr.rcuh.com/psp/hcmprd_exapp/EMPLOYEE/HRMS/c/HRS_HRAM.HRS_APP_SCHJOB.GBL?FOCUS=Applicant)
2. Search position **223315**
3. In your cover letter, address your desire to join the group and relocate to Hawaiʻi
