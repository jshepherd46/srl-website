# Services

The Shepherd Research Lab operates the **Body Composition Lab (BCEPEM)** as a core facility within the University of Hawaiʻi Cancer Center. BCEPEM specializes in Body Composition, Exercise Physiology, and Energy Metabolism — providing accurate and precise measures of physiological biomarkers for research and self-assessment.

## Modalities

### DXA Scan

Dual-energy X-ray Absorptiometry for body-composition analysis.  
[Learn more ↗](https://bcl.shepherdresearchlab.org/dxa-scan/)

### Bod Pod

Body composition and volume measurements using air displacement.  
[Learn more ↗](https://bcl.shepherdresearchlab.org/bodpod/)

### 3D Optical Scans

Full 3D image of the body with automatic circumference and length measurements.  
[Learn more ↗](https://bcl.shepherdresearchlab.org/3d-optical-scans/)

### Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis

Body analysis test using low-level currents.  
[Learn more ↗](https://bcl.shepherdresearchlab.org/bioelectrical-impedance-analysis/)

### Muscle Dynamometer

Measurement of muscle activation using isolated joint movements and different resistance modes.  
[Learn more ↗](https://bcl.shepherdresearchlab.org/muscle-dynamometer/)

### Metabolic Assessments

Tests for VO2max and Resting Metabolic Rate.  
[Learn more ↗](https://bcl.shepherdresearchlab.org/metabolic-assessment/)
